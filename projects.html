Product Overview
Its every organizations responsibility to ensure customer data is protected and managed effectively. To ensure and assess this, the AICPA (American Institute of Certified Public Accountants) developed certain frameworks. Most of these frameworks are designed to audit and provide compliant reports to organizations aiming to build trust with their customers, gain a competitive edge or enhance internal policies.
Product Users
The end users of this product are the consultants of a CPA firm who examine the organization's control and provide the reports.
Problem Statement
The consultants have to manually track each control with its associated point of focus for every new request (engagement) as there is no centralized system to manage it.
Product Objective
The aim is to have a product that:
1.  Maintains the relations between controls and the point of focuses that the controls address.
2.  Allow control description modifications.
3.  Allow control additions while ensuring they are mapped to specific point of focus(es).
4.  Allow control deletions by accepting a reasonable rationale.
5.  Have multiple review levels to ensure high quality outcome for each request.
6.  Allow cloning engagements at different levels.
7.  Generate reports.

Strategy & Execution
To build the product, we had a diverse team:
•  The business team of SMEs to provide their valuable insights of how the mappings should be linked.
•  The prod team to design prototypes and perform quality checks.
•  The design team to provide the aesthetics and ensure effective user experience.
•  The legal team to ensure we work according to the set procedures and are compliant to the policies.
Prioritization
The roadmap was created by ensuring that the end users receive the most effective features in the first release.
Methodology & Tools
For this project, we followed the Agile methodology to achieve accuracy in terms of meeting the desired requirements. The Scrum framework was chosen in order to consistently take feedback from the users and incorporate the desired changes in every iteration. The team used Azure DevOps to conduct the Scrum ceremonies.

Product Overview
Supply chain disruptions can have significant financial and operational impacts on organizations. The Supply Disruption Identifier (SDI) is a web-based application designed to proactively monitor global news and online articles to detect adverse events that may affect a company's suppliers. By automating the identification and categorization of disruptions, SDI empowers organizations to respond swiftly and mitigate risks across their supply chain.
Product Users
The primary users of SDI are:
•  Procurement and supply chain teams in Fortune 500 companies
•  Risk management professionals
•  Operations leaders seeking real-time supplier intelligence
Problem Statement
Organizations lack a scalable mechanism to monitor adverse mentions of their suppliers across the internet. Manual tracking is inefficient, reactive, and prone to oversight, especially when disruptions are buried in unstructured content.
Product Objective
SDI aims to:
1.  Extract articles from publicly accessible websites that permit data extraction.
2.  Detect mentions of suppliers using keyword matching and entity recognition.
3.  Identify adverse content that could impact supplier performance or reputation.
4.  Analyze article polarity to determine sentiment and severity.
5.  Categorize disruptions into predefined buckets such as Labor, Security, Financial, Environmental, etc.
6.  Display affected suppliers in a dynamic dashboard for decision-makers.
7.  Notify users when critical suppliers are flagged in adverse articles.

Strategy & Execution
To build SDI, we assembled a cross-functional team with deep expertise in supply chain, NLP, and enterprise software:
•  Business Team: Defined supplier keywords, disruption categories, and risk thresholds.
•  Product Team: Designed algorithms for supplier detection, sentiment analysis, and categorization.
•  Design Team: Created an intuitive dashboard to visualize supplier impact and article metadata.
•  Engineering Team: Built scalable backend pipelines for article ingestion and real-time analysis.
•  Legal & Compliance Team: Ensured ethical and compliant data extraction from public sources.
Prioritization
The MVP focused on:
•  Real-time dashboard of affected suppliers.
•  High-accuracy supplier matching.
•  Polarity scoring and disruption categorization.
Methodology & Tools
We adopted Agile methodology to ensure iterative development and stakeholder feedback incorporation:
•  Scrum framework enabled bi-weekly sprints, backlog grooming, and sprint reviews.
•  Azure DevOps: Used for sprint planning, task tracking, and release management.
•  Continuous Feedback Loop: Stakeholders reviewed dashboard prototypes and NLP outputs regularly.
•  Model Training: Leveraged pre-trained NLP models fine-tuned on supply chain-specific corporations.

Product Overview
Any asset's lifespan depends on its quality. To ensure consistent quality product is delivered to the end users, most products undergo rigorous testing. One of the type of testing is to ensure that the product doesn't deviate from its original purpose and quality standard; A repetitive set of test scripts - Regression suites. To accomplish this, the Script Automator was developed. It is a low-code automation tool designed to streamline the creation and maintenance of test scripts for web applications. It empowers users to generate test cases by either manually scraping DOM elements or using an intuitive click-and-record interface. Once elements are captured, users can edit and customize the test logic, enabling dynamic and reusable test flows. The asset reduces dependency on traditional scripting, accelerates QA cycles, and enhances test coverage with minimal manual effort.
Product Users
The asset is built for, but not limited to; QA Engineers (to automate repetitive test cases and reduce manual regression efforts) and Test Automation Architects (to design scalable test frameworks using reusable components).
Problem Statement
Manual test script creation is time-consuming, error-prone, and often requires specialized coding skills. As web applications evolve rapidly, maintaining test scripts becomes a bottleneck—leading to broken tests, delayed releases, and reduced confidence in product quality. Existing automation tools either demand high technical proficiency or lack flexibility in customizing test logic post-recording.
Product Objective
To deliver a user-friendly, flexible test automation asset that:
1.  Simplifies element capture through manual scraping or click-and-record.
2.  Enables users to edit and customize test logic using captured elements.
3.  Reduces test creation time and improves maintainability.
4.  Bridges the gap between technical and non-technical stakeholders in the QA process.
5.  Supports scalable, reusable test flows that adapt to evolving UI structures.

Strategy & Execution
A cross-functional team collaborated to build Script Automator:
•  Product team defined core use cases around test automation, element scraping, and script editing, ensuring alignment with QA pain points.
•  Engineering team developed the scraping engine and click-record interface, optimizing for speed and accuracy across dynamic web apps.
•  Design team crafted an intuitive UI for script editing and element management, enabling low-code accessibility for non-technical users.
•  QA team validated test script generation across multiple browsers and workflows, ensuring reliability and scalability.
•  Security & Compliance team reviewed data handling and DOM access protocols to ensure safe usage in enterprise environments.
Prioritization
Prioritization was guided by real-world QA workflows and feedback from early adopters. Feature roadmap was driven by user impact and technical feasibility:
•  Phase 1 focused on core automation: element scraping, click-record, and basic script editing.
•  Phase 2 introduced reusable components, conditional logic, and script versioning.
•  Phase 3 planned for CI/CD integration, test result dashboards, and role-based access control.
Methodology & Tools
Agile methodology was adopted to ensure iterative delivery and stakeholder alignment:
•  Scrum framework enabled bi-weekly sprints, backlog grooming, and sprint reviews.
•  Azure DevOps was used for work item tracking, code repositories, and automated build pipelines.
•  User feedback loops were embedded via sprint demos and usability testing sessions.
•  Test automation frameworks like Selenium and Playwright were evaluated for compatibility and extensibility.
